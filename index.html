    Water Aerator

    Fish depend on oxygen to breathe and sustain life. Without it, you risk your koi fish suffocating.

    Pond Setup

    Waters should be between 74-86 °F and the pH should be kept between 6-9. Kois need at least 250 gallons of water with a minimum depth of 6 feet.

    Diet

    Kois are omnivores and will eat seeds, plant material, algae, and even insects. You should keep a good stock of algae and floating weeds as well as commerical foods that are high in protein. Three portions of pellet food per day should suffice.




History of Koi

Origin

Koi fish or specifically named nishikigoi in Japanese are varieties of the Amur carp that were specifically bred and kept for decorative purposes. Carp was first bred for decorative purposes nearly a thousand years ago in China and eventually through selective breeding and various mutations came to the numerous patterns of koi we see today.

Meaning

The word koi is short for nishikigoi which literally means brocaded carp. As the name suggests they have been a symbol of strength and success for a long time. However, they really have been a symbol of most positive meanings such as good luck, prosperity, vitality, and longevity. Additionally, the word koi in Japanese means love.

Types

The three most common types are Kohaku, Taisho Sanke, and Showa. Kohaku has a white body with red splotches as the pattern. Taisho Sanke was developed from the Kohaku which just like the Kohaku has a white body with red splotches but additionally smaller black splotches. The Showa is similar to the Taisho Sanke except the black splotches are similar in size to the red splotches.
